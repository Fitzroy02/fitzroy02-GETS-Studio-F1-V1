from pirate radio to modern streaming platforms. Through case studies
of Islington depository projects, community radio stations, and 
ecological media initiatives, this book argues that locally-controlled
media is essential to democratic participation.

The book includes practical frameworks for establishing community
stations, managing ecological anchors, and balancing commercial
sustainability with civic mission. Features interviews with 50+
community broadcasters and media theorists from across the UK,
examining how grassroots media movements have shaped public discourse.

Special attention is given to the GETS Studio model, which integrates
streaming revenue with tree-planting initiatives and ocean conservation,
demonstrating how digital platforms can serve both creators and the
environment. The final chapters outline actionable strategies for
launching community stations with minimal funding while maintaining
editorial independence.
