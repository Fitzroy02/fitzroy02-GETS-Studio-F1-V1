## üìä Operational Flow: 7-Step Process

### **STEP 1: Artist Registration**

**Timeline:** Day 0 (Initial Setup)

**Actions:**
1. Artist completes registration form:
   - Full name, contact details, biography, portfolio links
   - Payment method setup (Direct Debit, card, or PayPal)
   - Agreement to ¬£10 monthly registration fee
   - Acceptance of Terms & Conditions

2. Platform processes registration:
   - Artist profile created with unique ID (format: `ARTIST-YYYYMMDD-XX-####`)
   - Monthly billing schedule established (enrollment date anniversary)
   - Artist dashboard activated with unlimited upload access

3. First payment processed:
   - ¬£10 monthly registration fee charged immediately
   - Access granted to upload interface, auction tools, eco-points tracking

**Key Points:**
- ¬£10/month covers unlimited artwork uploads (no per-listing fees)
- Registration required before any artwork can be submitted
- Late payment (>7 days overdue): Account suspended, listings paused
- Cancellation requires 30-day written notice

**Outputs:**
- Artist profile page (public-facing)
- Artist dashboard (private, with upload tools)
- Artist ID and certificate of registration
- Monthly billing schedule confirmation

### **STEP 2: Metadata Submission & Artwork Upload**

**Timeline:** Ongoing (anytime after registration)

**Actions:**
1. Artist completes metadata registration form for each artwork:

   **Required Information:**
   - Title, medium, year, dimensions, edition type
   - Description (100-500 words: concept, inspiration, technique, materials)
   - Condition report (current physical state, any repairs/damage)
   - Provenance/ownership history (if applicable)
   - Artist declaration (originality, copyright, compliance with excluded works policy)
   - Authenticity declaration (signed statement)

   **Auction Parameters:**
   - Sale type: Fixed price OR 4-week auction
   - If auction: Reserve price/minimum value (lowest accepted bid)
   - If auction: Starting bid (optional, can be lower than reserve)
   - Currency of sale (GBP default, EUR/USD/CAD supported)

   **Delivery Costs:**
   - Local delivery price (¬£) ‚Äî UK domestic
   - International delivery price (¬£) ‚Äî Europe/North America/Worldwide
   - Delivery method (carrier preference, packaging description)
   - Delivery timeframe (standard or custom)

   **Visual Assets:**
   - Primary high-resolution image (minimum 2000px, JPG/PNG/WEBP, max 10MB)
   - Additional images (optional: detail shots, alternate angles, context photos ‚Äî up to 5 images)

   **Compliance:**
   - Confirmation that artwork does not fall under excluded categories (mass-produced, purely AI-generated, reproduction, functional object, offensive/unlawful content, unverified)

2. Platform stores metadata:
   - All information catalogued in database
   - Blockchain verification prepared (SHA-256 hash)
   - Artwork assigned reference number (format: `ART-REG-YYYYMMDD-####`)

**Key Points:**
- Artists can upload unlimited artworks (covered by ¬£10/month registration)
- Each artwork requires complete metadata (checklist provided)
- Delivery costs must be specified upfront (local and international)
- Compliance declaration mandatory (protects platform and buyers)

**Outputs:**
- Artwork metadata record (stored in database)
- Artwork reference number
- Ready for curatorial review

### **STEP 3: Curatorial Review & Network Circulation**

**Timeline:** 1-3 business days (review) + 3-7 days (circulation/promotion)

**Actions:**
1. Curatorial team reviews submission:
   - **Evaluation criteria:**
     - Originality and artistic merit
     - Technical quality and image resolution
     - Authenticity and provenance verification
     - Compliance with content standards (excluded works check)
     - Commercial viability
     - Metadata completeness

2. Review decision:
   - **If approved:**
     - Email confirmation sent to artist
     - Artwork reference number and registration certificate issued (PDF download)
     - Certificate includes blockchain verification (SHA-256 hash)
     - Artwork moved to network circulation phase

   - **If rejected:**
     - Email with rejection reason and improvement suggestions
     - Artist may resubmit with revisions (within 30 days)
     - No fees charged for rejected submissions (monthly registration continues)

3. Network circulation begins (approved artworks):
   - **Promotion across platform channels:**
     - Featured on GETS Studio Main Gallery homepage
     - Social media posts (platform's 245k+ followers on Instagram, Twitter, Facebook)
     - Email newsletter to subscriber base (opt-in collectors, galleries, institutions)
     - Distribution to partner platforms (Saatchi Art, Artsy, Artfinder, etc.)

   - **Cataloguing and display:**
     - Artwork listing page created with full metadata
     - High-resolution images displayed in gallery grid
     - Eco-points display: "This sale plants [X] trees" (calculated from reserve/sale price)
     - For auctions: Countdown timer set for 4-week duration

4. Artist notified:
   - Gallery listing URL provided
   - For fixed-price: "Live now, buyers can purchase immediately"
   - For auction: "Auction start date [Date], bidding opens in [X] days"

**Key Points:**
- Curatorial review ensures quality and compliance
- Network circulation builds visibility before auction starts
- Promotion at no additional cost to artist (covered by monthly registration)
- Blockchain verification adds authenticity and trust

**Outputs:**
- Approved artwork goes live on platform
- Certificate of registration issued (blockchain-verified)
- Promotional campaign launched across channels
- For auctions: 4-week countdown timer activated

### **STEP 4: Auction Period (4-Week Bidding Cycle)**

**Timeline:** Fixed 4 weeks from auction start date (28 days)

**Actions:**

**Week 1: Auction Opens**
1. Bidding window opens at set start date/time
2. Starting bid (or reserve price) displayed on artwork page
3. Countdown timer shows: "27 days, XX hours remaining"
4. Buyers can register and place bids
5. Each bid must exceed current highest bid by minimum increment (¬£10-¬£50 depending on artwork value)
6. Real-time bid tracking: Highest bidder displayed (username anonymized: "Bidder A," "Bidder B")
7. Eco-points display updates with each bid: "Current bid: ¬£500 ‚Üí 5 trees will be planted"

**Week 2-3: Active Bidding Period**
1. Bidding activity tracked and promoted
2. Email notifications sent to bidders if outbid: "You've been outbid on [Artwork Title]. Place a higher bid to stay in the lead."
3. Artist can monitor bidding activity via dashboard (real-time updates)
4. Platform promotes artwork with bid updates: "Bidding active! Current price ¬£750 ‚Üí 7 trees"
5. Buyers can set maximum bid (proxy bidding): Platform auto-bids up to maximum on buyer's behalf

**Week 4: Final Countdown**
1. Urgency messaging: "Only 3 days left!" / "Final 24 hours!"
2. Last-minute bidding often increases (auction psychology)
3. Countdown timer displays days ‚Üí hours ‚Üí minutes ‚Üí seconds
4. Platform sends final reminder emails to registered bidders
5. Artist receives notification: "Auction closes in 24 hours, current bid ¬£1,200"

**Auction Close (4-Week Mark)**
1. Bidding window closes at exact 4-week timestamp
2. System automatically determines winner:
   - **If reserve met:** Highest bidder wins
   - **If reserve NOT met:** Artwork does not sell (see Step 4B below)

3. Winning bidder receives immediate notification:
   - "Congratulations! You won [Artwork Title] for ¬£1,200"
   - "Payment due within 48 hours: ¬£1,200 (final bid) + ¬£24 (2% admin fee) + ¬£25 (local delivery) = ¬£1,249 total"
   - Payment instructions and secure checkout link provided

4. Artist receives notification:
   - "Your artwork sold for ¬£1,200! Settlement in progress."
   - "You will receive ¬£840 (70% of ¬£1,176 after admin fee) within 7 business days"
   - "Eco-points earned: 120 points ‚Üí 12 trees will be planted"

**Key Points:**
- Auction duration fixed at 4 weeks (cannot be extended)
- Real-time bidding with transparent highest bid display
- Eco-points display encourages civic-minded bidding
- Reserve price protects artist from underselling
- 48-hour payment window after auction close

**Outputs:**
- Final bid amount determined
- Winning bidder identified
- Settlement process initiated (if reserve met)
- Eco-points calculated and logged

### **STEP 4B: Reserve Not Met (Alternative Path)**

**Timeline:** Immediately after auction close (if reserve price not reached)

**Actions:**
1. Auction closes with highest bid below reserve
2. System marks artwork as "Reserve not met ‚Äî Did not sell"
3. Notifications sent:
   - **To artist:** "Your artwork did not reach the reserve price of ¬£1,000. Highest bid was ¬£850. What would you like to do?"
   - **To highest bidder:** "Thank you for bidding on [Artwork Title]. Unfortunately, the reserve price was not met and the artwork is not available for sale at this time."

4. Artist options presented:
   - **Option A: Relist for another 4-week auction**
     - Keep same reserve price OR adjust reserve (lower to encourage sale, or higher if confident)
     - New auction starts immediately or on scheduled date
     - Promoted as "Relisted" with previous bidding history visible (builds confidence)

   - **Option B: Convert to fixed-price listing**
     - Set fixed price (typically at or near reserve)
     - Buyers can purchase immediately without bidding
     - Remains listed until sold or artist removes

   - **Option C: Remove from platform**
     - Artwork taken down from gallery
     - Metadata retained in artist's private archive
     - Can be re-uploaded later if desired

5. No fees charged if artwork does not sell:
   - No admin fee (only charged on successful sales)
   - Monthly ¬£10 registration fee continues (covers platform access regardless of sales)

**Key Points:**
- Reserve price protects artist from selling below desired value
- Flexible options after unsuccessful auction (relist, fixed-price, remove)
- No penalty or additional fees for unsold artwork
- Artist maintains full control over pricing and listing decisions

**Outputs:**
- Artwork marked "Did not sell"
- Artist chooses next action (relist, convert, or remove)
- No revenue distribution (no sale completed)

### **STEP 5: Sale & Settlement**

**Timeline:** 48 hours (buyer payment) + 7 business days (artist payout)

**Actions:**

**Within 48 Hours of Auction Close:**
1. Winning bidder completes payment:
   - Final bid amount: ¬£1,200 (example)
   - Administrative fee (2%): ¬£24
   - Delivery cost (local or international): ¬£25 (local example)
   - **Total due from buyer:** ¬£1,249

2. Payment methods accepted:
   - Credit/Debit Card (Visa, Mastercard, Amex)
   - PayPal
   - Bank Transfer (for high-value purchases over ¬£5,000)

3. Platform processes payment:
   - Payment gateway (Stripe/PayPal) confirms transaction
   - Funds held in platform escrow account pending delivery
   - Buyer receives payment confirmation email with:
     - Transaction receipt
     - Tracking number (once artist ships)
     - Certificate of authenticity (PDF, blockchain-verified)
     - Ecological impact statement: "Your purchase plants 12 trees and contributes ¬£48 to ocean conservation"

**If Payment Not Received Within 48 Hours:**
1. Reminder email sent to winning bidder at 24-hour mark
2. Final warning at 40-hour mark: "Payment overdue, artwork will be offered to next highest bidder"
3. If still no payment at 48-hour mark:
   - Winning bidder disqualified (account warning issued, repeat offenses result in suspension)
   - Artwork offered to second-highest bidder (if within 10% of winning bid and above reserve)
   - If no suitable backup bidder: Artwork relisted or converted to fixed-price (artist's choice)

**Artist Shipment (Within 5-10 Business Days):**
1. Artist receives shipment notification:
   - "Payment confirmed! Please ship artwork to [Buyer Address] within 5-10 business days"
   - Packaging guidelines provided (secure wrapping, insurance, tracking required)
   - Shipping label and insurance can be arranged through platform (optional service)

2. Artist ships artwork:
   - Uses tracked and insured delivery method (Royal Mail, DHL, FedEx, UPS, specialist art courier)
   - Provides tracking number to platform (entered via artist dashboard)
   - Platform forwards tracking number to buyer via email

3. Platform holds funds in escrow until delivery confirmed

**Delivery Confirmation:**
1. Buyer receives artwork and tracking shows "Delivered"
2. Buyer must confirm receipt within 48 hours (automated reminder sent)
3. If no confirmation, delivery considered complete after 72 hours (auto-confirmation)
4. 7-day viewing/refund period begins upon confirmation (see Step 6)

**Revenue Distribution (Within 7 Business Days of Delivery Confirmation):**
1. Platform calculates revenue distribution:
   - Final bid: ¬£1,200
   - Administrative fee (2%): ¬£24 (retained by platform)
   - Amount for distribution: ¬£1,176

2. Artist payment processed:
   - ¬£823.20 transferred to artist's designated account (bank transfer or PayPal)
   - Payment confirmation email with breakdown:
     - Gross sale: ¬£1,200
     - Admin fee (2%): -¬£24
     - Your share (70% of ¬£1,176): ¬£823.20
     - Platform operations (20%): ¬£235.20

**Key Points:**

### **STEP 6: Refund Window (Buyer Protection)**

**Timeline:** 7 days from viewing/delivery confirmation (to request refund) + 48 hours after item return and platform notification (for refund processing)

**Actions:**

**Viewing Period Begins:**
1. **"Date of viewing"** defined as:
   - **Physical delivery:** Date buyer receives and unwraps artwork (tracked delivery confirmation or buyer's written acknowledgment)
   - **Digital delivery:** Date buyer downloads high-resolution file
   - **In-person pickup:** Date buyer collects artwork and confirms receipt

2. 7-day countdown starts from viewing date
3. Buyer has right to inspect artwork and compare to listing description

**Refund Eligibility:**
Buyer may request refund if:
- Artwork significantly differs from description (color, size, condition)
- Artwork damaged during shipping (must be documented with photos within 48 hours of delivery)
- Authenticity concerns (artwork not created by listed artist)
- Quality issues not disclosed in listing (tears, stains, structural damage)

**Non-Refundable Reasons:**
- Buyer dissatisfaction unrelated to description accuracy (e.g., "changed my mind," "doesn't match my decor")
- Buyer's remorse or change of taste
- Damage caused by buyer after delivery

**Refund Request Process:**
1. Buyer contacts platform support within 7-day viewing period:
   - Email: buyers@getsstudio.com
   - Subject: "Refund Request ‚Äî Artwork [Reference Number]"
   - Include reason and photographic evidence (if applicable)

2. Platform reviews request within 48 hours:
   - Customer support team evaluates claim
   - Reviews original listing description, images, condition report
   - Contacts artist for response/evidence/defense
   - Makes decision based on Terms & Conditions

3. **If refund approved:**
   - Buyer returns artwork to artist in original condition
   - Buyer notifies platform when item has been returned (tracking number or confirmation of receipt by artist)
   - Buyer pays return shipping costs (unless damage occurred during original shipping due to artist's inadequate packaging)
   - Platform processes refund within **48 hours after the item is returned and the platform has been notified**
   - Full purchase price refunded to buyer's original payment method (final bid + admin fee)
   - **Delivery costs non-refundable** (buyer bears original and return shipping costs)

4. **If refund denied:**
   - Platform provides detailed explanation citing Terms & Conditions
   - Buyer may appeal decision within 7 days (senior management review)
   - If still denied: Buyer's only recourse is dispute resolution process (mediation, arbitration, litigation)

**Refund Distribution (If Approved):**
1. Platform refunds buyer:
   - Final bid: ¬£1,200
   - Admin fee (2%): ¬£24
   - **Total refunded to buyer:** ¬£1,224
   - Delivery costs: NOT refunded (¬£25 local example)

2. Platform recoups funds:
   - From artist's pending earnings: ¬£823.20 (70% previously paid)
   - From platform operational allocation: ¬£235.20 (20% absorbed by platform)
   - **Ecological allocation remains permanent:** ¬£117.60 NOT recouped (trees already planted, ocean funds already distributed ‚Äî ecological commitment is irreversible)

3. Financial impact:
   - Buyer recovers purchase price (minus delivery costs)
   - Artist loses sale revenue (must return ¬£823.20)
   - Platform absorbs operational costs (¬£235.20 loss)
   - Trees remain planted (ecological anchor permanent)

**Artist Protection:**
1. If refund due to buyer's misrepresentation or damage caused by buyer, refund may be denied
2. Artist notified immediately of refund requests and can provide evidence/defense
3. False or fraudulent refund claims result in buyer account suspension
4. Platform mediates disputes fairly and neutrally

**Key Points:**
- 7-day viewing period to request refund (from delivery/viewing date)
- Refunds only granted if artwork not as described or damaged in shipping
- Buyer must return item AND notify platform before refund processing begins
- Refund processed within 48 hours after return confirmation and platform notification
- Delivery costs non-refundable (buyer bears shipping risk)
- Ecological allocation permanent (trees planted regardless of refund)
- Platform absorbs operational costs if refund approved (protects artist)

**Outputs:**
- Refund processed within 48 hours after item returned and platform notified (if approved)
- Buyer receives purchase price back (minus delivery)
- Artist returns payment received
- Ecological anchors remain funded (trees already planted)

### **STEP 7: Eco-Points Allocation & Ecological Impact**

**Timeline:** Immediate calculation, quarterly tree planting, annual reporting

**Actions:**

**Immediate Eco-Points Calculation:**
1. At sale completion, platform automatically calculates eco-points:
   - **Eco-points = 10% of final sale value** (the ecological allocation amount)
   - Example: ¬£1,200 final bid ‚Üí ¬£120 ecological allocation ‚Üí **120 eco-points**

2. Conversion rate:
   - **1,000 eco-points = 1 tree planted**
   - Example: 120 eco-points ‚Üí 0.12 trees (accumulates toward 1 full tree at 1,000 points)

3. Eco-points credited to artist's profile:
   - Real-time update in artist dashboard
   - Public display on artist's gallery page: "This artist has planted 47 trees through sales"
   - Cumulative total tracked across all artwork sales

**Ecological Allocation Breakdown:**
1. **60% to Reforestation (¬£70.56 from ¬£117.60 example):**
   - **¬£10 = 1 native tree planted**
   - Example: ¬£70.56 ‚Üí 7 trees (rounded down from 7.056)
   - **Tree species:** Oak, birch, rowan, Scots pine, hazel, willow (native UK); region-appropriate species for international projects
   - **Planting partners:** Woodland Trust, Trees for Life, National Forest Company, One Tree Planted
   - **Locations:** Scotland, Wales, Northern England, Cornwall (UK); international reforestation zones (where applicable)
   - **CO‚ÇÇ offset:** 0.1 tonnes per tree over 10-year lifespan (7 trees = 0.7 tonnes CO‚ÇÇ)

2. **40% to Ocean Conservation (¬£47.04 from ¬£117.60 example):**
   - **Funds distributed to:** Ocean Cleanup (plastic removal from Pacific Garbage Patch), Surfers Against Sewage (UK coastal cleanup), Marine Conservation Society (coral restoration, marine protected areas)
   - **Projects supported:**
     - Plastic collection operations
     - Beach and coastal cleanups
     - Coral nursery programs
     - Marine protected area enforcement
     - Ocean acidification research

**Quarterly Tree Planting:**
1. Platform accumulates ecological allocation funds quarterly (Jan-Mar, Apr-Jun, Jul-Sep, Oct-Dec)
2. At end of each quarter, trees planted in batch:
   - Coordinates with planting partners (Woodland Trust, Trees for Life, etc.)
   - Tree planting locations documented (GPS coordinates, species, date)
   - Photographs taken and shared with artists and buyers

3. Quarterly Eco Impact Certificates issued to all artists:
   - **Certificate ID:** `ECO-IMP-YYYYMMDD-####`
   - **Contents:**
     - Artist name and Artist ID
     - Reporting period (e.g., Q4 2025: October-December)
     - Total artwork sales this quarter (number and value)

4. Certificate delivery:
   - PDF download via artist dashboard
   - Email notification with shareable digital certificate
   - Artist can display certificate on portfolio, social media, website

**Engagement-Based Eco-Points (Platform-Wide):**
1. **10,000 likes/favorites = 1 tree planted** (funded by platform's operational allocation)
2. Likes counted cumulatively across entire platform (all artworks, all artists)
3. Quarterly tallies and planting campaigns:

3. Report distribution:

2. **Third-party audits:** Annual independent audit by environmental assessors (verifies tree planting, ocean fund distribution, calculations)
3. **Public accountability:** Quarterly and annual reports published openly (anyone can verify platform's ecological claims)
