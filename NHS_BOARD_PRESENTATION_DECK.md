Q1: What if patients/staff don't own compatible watch devices?
A: We can supply clinical-grade heart monitors via Bluetooth/HL7
   integration. Also supports hospital bedside monitors. Watch
   devices are preferred but not required.

Q2: How do we ensure clinical teams respond to alerts promptly?
A: Multi-channel alerting (SMS/email/dashboard/pager). Alert
   escalation protocols configured during setup. On-call
   integration supported.

Q3: What happens if we want to expand beyond 500 beds mid-pilot?
A: Simple amendment to license. Pro-rata calculation for
   additional beds. No long procurement delay.

Q4: Can we pause the license if we face budget constraints?
A: Yes. 30-day notice required. Pro-rata refund for unused
   portion available (Tier 2/3). We're flexible and supportive.

Q5: What if the pilot doesn't deliver expected outcomes?
A: Evaluation at Month 6 and Month 12. If targets not met, we
   work with you to optimize or conclude pilot. No obligation
   to renew if outcomes aren't achieved.

Q6: How does this integrate with our existing EHR (Epic/Cerner)?
A: HL7 FHIR API integration (included in Tier 2/3). Works with
   Epic, Cerner, EMIS, SystmOne, and most NHS systems.

Q7: What about GDPR and patient consent?
A: Full GDPR compliance. Patients provide informed consent before
   monitoring starts. Right to withdraw consent (data deleted
   within 48 hours). DPIA completed and available.

Q8: Who owns the data generated during the pilot?
A: Your Trust owns all clinical data. We process data on your
   behalf (data processor role). Data residency: UK only.

Q9: Can we use pilot data for research/publications?
A: Yes, with appropriate ethics approval. Anonymized data
   available for research (Tier 3). We support academic
   partnerships.

Q10: What's the exit strategy if we decide not to continue?
A: 30-day notice. Data export provided (CSV/JSON formats).
   Integration removed. No vendor lock-in. Clean exit process.
